Hackathon!

Presenter: TBA
Organisers: Antoine Loirette (University of Glasgow) ; Kyle Martin (Robert Gordon University) ; Simone Barlocco (University of Strathclyde) ;
RDF Domain: A3 - Creativity; B3 - Professional and career development; C3 - Finance, funding and resources; D1 - Working with others;

Lecture slides will become available here

Abstract

The PhD Conference Hackathon offers a unique opportunity for research students to learn the skills required to submit successful grant application to funding bodies. The goal of the Hackathon is to help research students develop these skills and encourage cross-theme and cross-institution collaboration.

Session Outline

  • Part 1 (90 minutes): Part 1 (90 min): Students will receive a presentation upon how to write a good grant proposal from a SICSA expert.
  • Part 2 (90 minutes):In this second slot and over the course of lunch time (if they would like to), students will work in groups to construct a short research proposal which combines their study areas, with the goal of proposing a novel research project.

Learning Outcomes

  • LO1: Students will learn how to submit successful grant applications.
  • LO2: Students will write two-page summary of an ideal grant.
  • LO3: Students will prepare a 5-minute presentation of their work.

Additional Information

Capacity: 48 people (12 teams of four)

Time allotment: 3 Hours (2 x 1H 30Mins sessions)

Resources: Computer per person, large flip chart paper with pens.
